One of the most frequently asked questions translation professionals receive is: "Can you add the Hague Apostille for me?" Here's an important clarification: We wish we could, but the Apostille is not a translator's stamp! Think of the Apostille as an "authenticity certification" of the signature of the official who issued the original document. It is a government procedure that must be completed before the document reaches our hands. We then translate the content of the document along with the already existing Hague Apostille, so that the final result is complete.

## The Digital Transition: e-Apostille

To make things easier for citizens, a large part of this process has now been digitized. Through the e-apostille.gov.gr platform, you can request and receive the Hague Apostille electronically for several categories of documents. This significantly simplifies the steps, as once you receive the digital file, you can forward it to us immediately for its official translation, without needing to physically visit public services.

## What Is the Correct Order of Steps?

To avoid unnecessary hassle, the recommended workflow is as follows: First, confirm with the authority where you will submit the document whether the Hague Apostille is required. If so, arrange for its issuance from the competent authority (or via e-apostille). The final step is the official translation. By following this order, you ensure that your document will be fully valid and ready for use.

In short, the Hague Apostille is your document's "passport" for use abroad, and our job is to give it a "voice" in the language you need.
